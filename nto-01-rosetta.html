The Engagement

Northern Trail Outfitters, in two voices.

NTO is a fictional retailer that ships in every Salesforce demo for a reason — it has the shape of a real engagement: a CMO with a unification problem, a Head of Customer Service with a deflection problem, four operational systems (CRM, Demandware, Marketing Cloud, POS), partial loyalty coverage, an unresolved data-warehouse question, and a stated intent to deploy agentic experiences before the data is ready.

The discovery dossier is seven Word documents — two business scenarios (CMO voice + Head of Service voice), a discovery framework, a data-readiness guide, and three execution scenarios that play out the same use case three different ways. Per Manual Chapter 8 doctrine, Phase 1 captures Business Intent verbatim — no interpretation. What follows are the actual stakeholder voices the plugin pulled from the dossier.

CMO Voice — Tier-Based Customer Growth

At NTO, we've grown rapidly across digital and in-store channels, but our ability to truly understand and engage our customers has not kept pace. Today, our customer data is fragmented across commerce, marketing, and service systems. As a result, we are not consistently recognizing our most valuable customers, nor are we effectively guiding others toward higher-value relationships with our brand.

My priority is to increase revenue and customer lifetime value by:
  • Identifying and deeply understanding our top-tier customers
  • Engaging mid-tier customers with personalized experiences that increase their loyalty and spend
  • Ensuring every interaction — whether marketing or service — is relevant, timely, and context-aware
At the same time, we are investing in agentic experiences to scale customer engagement. However, these experiences must be grounded in trusted, complete, and current data, or we risk damaging customer trust. Source — 1.A Business Scenario (CMO/CDP) §1

Use case

Tier-Based Customer Growth and Intelligent Engagement.

Business action

Identify Tier 2 customers with high potential and engage them with personalized, context-aware experiences to increase their spend and move them into Tier 1.

Success metrics

% Tier 2 → Tier 1 promotion; AOV increase; repeat purchase frequency; campaign conversion rates; revenue contribution by tier.

Head of Service Voice — 24/7 Case Deflection

At NTO, customer expectations have evolved faster than our ability to support them at scale. Customers expect fast, accurate, and always-available support, whether they are checking an order, resolving an issue, or deciding whether to purchase again. Today, we rely heavily on human agents, which creates challenges:
  • Limited availability outside business hours
  • Inconsistent response quality depending on the agent
  • Increasing case volumes driving longer wait times
At the same time, our customer data is spread across multiple systems. This makes it difficult for agents — human or digital — to quickly understand the full context of a customer's situation. Source — 1.B Business Scenario (Customer Service) §1

Use case

24/7 Case Deflection and Intelligent Customer Service.

Business action

Allow customers to self-serve common support needs through an AI agent, while ensuring the agent can accurately identify the customer, understand their context, and provide correct, consistent responses.

Success metrics

% cases successfully deflected; average response time; CSAT; first-contact resolution rate; reduction in AHT; escalation accuracy.

A doctrinal claim shared by both stakeholders

Both voices state the same precondition for success: "these experiences must be grounded in trusted, complete, and current data." This is the load-bearing claim of the entire engagement. Phase 3's verdict will measure whether NTO is, in fact, ready to host such experiences. The answer turns out to be not yet, and the plugin is willing to say so honestly.

The Methodology

Four phases. Five gates. One discipline contract.

Project Archaeology v1.3 is the methodology Vernon Keenan and the Rosetta team developed for excavating a Digital Twin from messy brownfield evidence. It defines four phases, four governance gates, an evidence taxonomy with seven classes, an eleven-dimension readiness rubric, and a v1.3 DecisionTrace schema with three mandatory blocks. The plugin operationalizes that doctrine.

Phase 1
Survey
Establish domain placement. Capture verbatim Business Intent. Map org topology. Survey canonical inheritance candidates.
Phase 2
Excavation
Inventory every evidence item by class. Build the Site Package. Catalog anti-patterns. Document what's not here.
Phase 3
Classification
Three-layer Context Artifacts. v1.3 DecisionTraces with mandatory negative criteria. The eleven-dimension readiness verdict.
Phase 4
Preservation
Identify PromotionCandidates. Generate the partner certification request for Gate 3 review. Stage validation checklist.

The non-negotiables

The plugin enforces three doctrinal contracts at hard gates. Skipping them is not allowed.

Contract What it means Where the plugin enforces it
Negative criteria are mandatory Every DecisionTrace's validation.negativeCriteria must be non-empty. An empty list is a hard fail per Manual Chapter 7 Principle 1. The plugin refuses to advance Phase 3 if any DecisionTrace has empty negative criteria. Phase 3 static analysis check 2; refuses to build the bundle until satisfied.
Tooling symmetry Every DecisionTrace's alternativesConsidered must have at least 2 entries (the selected alternative plus at least one rejected). "No alternatives considered" must be stated explicitly with rationale, not implicitly via a single entry. Phase 3 schema validation; ≥2 alternatives per trace.
Existing config is evidence, not truth What's in an org is evidence of past activity, not a verdict on current correctness. Confidence cannot exceed Medium without cross-class corroboration. Phase 2 evidence-taxonomy classification; Phase 3 confidence bounding.

Combined, these contracts produce a Digital Twin where every claim is traceable to its evidence, every decision shows its rejected alternatives, and every absence-of-evidence is annotated rather than papered over.

Phase 2 disclosure — what's NOT here

A dossier-only excavation has eight known forensic gaps: no live org metadata snapshot; no actual data-catalog scan output; no live integration trace; no live permission-model audit; no real Cuneiform pass; no actual interview transcripts; no live order-volume counts; no actual platform-feature inventory.

These eight gaps are expected for a worked-example excavation. They are unacceptable for a real engagement headed to Phase 3 readiness verdict. Phase 3 cannot issue "Ready" against the eleven-dimension rubric on the basis of practitioner testimony plus authoritative guidance alone. This single methodological discipline — refusing to issue an unbounded "Ready" verdict — is what separates Project Archaeology from generic AI-readiness assessments.

Phase 3 · Classification — The Keystone

The Digital Twin takes shape.

Phase 3 is the methodological climax. It produces three-layer Context Artifacts (intent, structure, binding) for each functional domain, externalizes DecisionTraces from trajectory evidence using the forensic-extraction rule, and renders the eleven-dimension readiness verdict — for a specific intended agentic action, not a generic quality assessment.

DT-NTO01-001
Identity Resolution Rule Set
decision_type: pattern-selection
urgency: high · reversibility: medium
Decision question

What identity-resolution rule set should NTO use to unify Individuals across Salesforce CRM, Demandware, Marketing Cloud, and POS, given Cuneiform-cited contact-point reliability gaps?

Discovery

NTO-cited Cuneiform findings (Email ~75%, BusinessPhone ~69%, duplicates ~4%) make dossier-claimed deterministic match on email alone insufficient. CL-042 provides the canonical pattern but with default settings that assume higher contact-point quality than NTO has.

Decision · selected pattern

CL-042-refined-for-NTO with overrideMode refine: require email + loyalty ID dual-key deterministic match where loyalty ID is present, gate fuzzy fallback by per-field value-quality thresholds, preserve source-system lineage on every UnifiedIndividual.

Alternatives considered (3)

1. CL-042 base (no refinement, accept canonical defaults) — rejected per ap-101..105. 2. CL-042 with refine (selected) — only option that survives the anti-pattern catalog. 3. Replace CL-042 with a custom identity service — rejected for now; carried as evidenceGap for Phase 4.

Positive criteria (4)
  • Identity resolution must use email + loyalty ID as dual-key deterministic match where loyalty ID is present.
  • Fuzzy fallback must be gated by per-field value-quality thresholds derived from a current data-catalog scan.
  • Every UnifiedIndividual must carry source-system lineage attribution for each contributing record.
  • Resolution accuracy must be measurable via held-out test set with documented Pass@k.
Negative criteria (5) — derived from forensic-extraction rule
  • Must not use email alone as deterministic match (ap-203 from Scenario 2).
  • Must not assume contact-point fields are reliable on the basis of population rate alone (ap-102).
  • Must not treat Snowflake as authoritative for cross-system identity (ap-202).
  • Must not promote this refinement to the salesforce-fde partner domain without Phase 4 partner certification.
  • Must not present a single-source testimony (the CMO voice) as corroborated evidence for tier definitions downstream.

Why not "Conditionally Ready"

Per Manual Chapter 6, "Conditionally Ready" means met for the specified action subject to specific, named conditions. The Identity-resolution and Data-quality dimensions do not yet meet the specified action — they fail. The remediation is feasible, hence "Remediable" rather than "Structural", but failure is failure: claiming "Conditionally Ready" against unmeasured contact-point reliability would reproduce ap-105 (Looks Unified ≡ Trusted). This is the methodology eating its own dogfood — the anti-pattern catalog from Phase 2 is being used as a check on Phase 3's own verdict-shaping.

The Dependency Map

One root cause, two failure cascades.

The Manual is explicit: "These eleven dimensions are not a checklist. A readiness assessment is a graph of dependencies between them." The map below shows why the verdict is identical for the CDP action and the Agentforce action — they share the load-bearing dependencies.

Reading the map: Data quality (3) is the upstream root; Identity resolution (7) is the choke point that bounds both downstream actions; Consent (5) gates CDP activation; Permission model (4) is the cross-cutting blind spot that must be closed before any production cutover. One root cause produces the whole readiness story.

Phase 4 · The Gate 3 Hand-off

What the partner architect actually receives.

Gate 3 (Partner Validation) is the methodological gate the plugin cannot perform — only a senior partner architect with field experience can certify whether a candidate "matches how decisions actually got made in the field." The plugin's job is to produce a usable handoff: a concise reviewer brief and a structured way to file the verdict.

PARTNER-CERTIFICATION-REQUEST.md (excerpt)

The reviewer must be a senior architect inside the target partner domain for the candidate under review. For two of the three candidates that target domain is salesforce-fde, the Salesforce Forward-Deployed Engineer practice. For one candidate the target domain is salesforce:rosetta-core and the reviewer must be a member of the Rosetta core engineering team or an architect with explicit core-domain authoring rights.

The reviewer must have personal field experience with the kind of engagement the candidate abstracts from. For DT-NTO01-001 and DT-NTO01-003 that means hybrid-commerce or warehouse- present customer-data-unification engagements. For DT-NTO01-002 that means tier or segment or scoring-driven engagements where downstream activation depends on upstream identity resolution.

The reviewer must disclose any conflict of interest before recording a verdict. Conflicts include direct authorship of the originating engagement, financial interest in a vendor whose product appears in the candidate, or a prior promotion of an inconsistent pattern that this candidate would supersede.

The three verdict states

The reviewer files one of three verdicts per candidate, each with a deterministic downstream consequence:

Verdict Meaning Consequence
Faithful The reviewer has direct field experience with the decision the candidate captures, and the externalized DecisionTrace matches how that decision actually got made in the field. Proceeds to Gate 4 (governance approval) with partner certification appended to the provenance trail.
Partial-faithful The reviewer recognizes the underlying decision but the externalized DecisionTrace omits, mischaracterizes, or wrongly emphasizes a material element. Returns to engagement for amendment. Reviewer must enumerate the specific amendments required.
Not-faithful The reviewer cannot confirm the trace from direct experience and judges the decision either did not happen as captured or is not generalizable. Returns to engagement for re-investigation. Trace remains valid for the original engagement but does not promote.

Without partner certification, no candidate proceeds past Gate 3 to governance review. This is the v1.3 gate that prevents unfalsifiable reconstructions from rubber-stamping into ADRs. The methodological discipline transfer the Manual demands lives in the architect's willingness to certify against personal experience, not against the documented evidence.

Architectural Decisions Ahead

The questions this demo surfaces.

The most valuable artifact in the bundle is the Phase 4 VALIDATION-CHECKLIST, because it does not just hand the user a list of make commands — it surfaces the architectural decisions Salesforce engineering needs to make before NTO-02 happens. These are real questions, not plugin bugs.

  1. How does engagement-scoped content enter the canonical build pipeline? The db/engagements/<engagementKey>/ path is a v1.3 convention introduced by this plugin. As of rosetta-platform-0.1.1, the existing make seed-compile and make db-seed targets do not scan it. Three options: manual cypher-shell load (lowest effort), extend SEED_FILES in scripts/lib/env.sh (commingles canonical and engagement content), or add a parallel db-engagement-load target (recommended for v1.3, supports the supersession path). Pick one before NTO-02.
  2. Inline maps vs. child nodes for alternativesConsidered and provenanceSources? The plugin template specified separate Alternative and ProvenanceEntry child nodes. Phase 3 produced a hybrid: child nodes for provenance, inline properties for alternatives. Both are queryable, but pick one and pin it for cross-plugin consistency with rosetta-research.
  3. What is the partner-domain projectId convention? Phase 1 set partner.projectId = 'salesforce-fde' (mirroring the partner key). Should this be 'SYSTEM' instead, since the partner-level domain is canonical inheritable content rather than engagement-scoped? A small architectural question with downstream implications for invariant 13.3 grep queries.
  4. Do partner-level KnowledgeDomain nodes carry overrideMode? Manual Chapter 12 specifies five-field identity on every inheritable node. The leaf engagement domain takes overrideMode = 'base' as a novel node. The convention for partner-level domains (salesforce-fde as a child of salesforce:rosetta-core) needs explicit pinning.

None of these questions block NTO-01 from being a valid demonstration of the methodology. All of them block NTO-02 from being a smooth replication. The right time to resolve them is now — between this NTO-01 steel thread and the next engagement.
