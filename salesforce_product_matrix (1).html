⚠ Key Structural Issues (from Rosetta Content Inventory)

🔥 DATA360 is a Fiction

The Agentforce 360 page positions Data 360 as an umbrella containing Informatica, Tableau, and MuleSoft. But clicking the Data 360 link goes to salesforce.com/data/ — which is just Data Cloud. Informatica, Tableau, and MuleSoft are separately sold, separately priced products. The diagram conflates "what works with Data Cloud" with "what IS Data Cloud".

🔥 $10 Challenge: Agentforce vs Agentforce 360 vs Salesforce AI

Even internally, no one can clearly distinguish these three brand names. The homepage says "Salesforce brings together Slack, Agentforce, Customer 360, and Data 360" — Agentforce 360 doesn't appear here at all. These three brands appear to be used interchangeably across different pages on salesforce.com with no consistent definition.

⚠ salesforce.com Has Two Different Navs

The Service sub-menu on the main products page differs from the same nav on the Agentforce 360 page. "Field Service" → "Field Service and Operations", "Self-Service" → "Service Portal", and two items (Digital Engagement, Voice & Phone Support) are completely removed. Multiple teams appear to control different nav configurations within the same domain.

⚠ 17 Industries Nav, 16 Pricing Blocks

There are 17 industry sections consistently in the salesforce.com nav. The pricing page only has 16 blocks — Healthcare and Life Sciences are combined into one. These are separately licensed and separately priced products. The pricing page disguises this split.

ℹ Quip: $750M Acquisition — Invisible on Salesforce

Quip (acquired 2016 for ~$750M) has its own site at quip.com with pricing and a developer portal. It is completely absent from the salesforce.com main nav, the pricing page, and every Salesforce property except Trailhead. A major Salesforce product that prospects cannot discover from the main website.

ℹ Blog Fragmentation: 15+ Separate Properties

Salesforce content is split across: 360 Blog, Small Biz blog, Developer blog, Admin blog, Architect blog, Engineering blog, Design blog, AI Research, Salesforce+ (video), MuleSoft blog, Tableau blog, Slack blog, Heroku Dev Center, AppExchange blog, Trailhead, security.salesforce.com, plus legacy Medium accounts. No unified search across all properties.

◈ Help Site Has Near-Zero Coverage for New Products

help.salesforce.com product documentation counts: Data 360 = 2 docs · Agentforce = 1 doc · Experience Cloud = 2 docs · "Additional Products" = random list of 64 docs. The JS SPA makes it difficult to crawl, but the doc inventory confirms that the help site has not kept pace with the new product naming or release cadence.

◈ Informatica: Partner, Not Product

Informatica appears in the salesforce.com Platform nav alongside native Salesforce products but has NO pricing page entry and its own separate website (informatica.com). The May 2025 acquisition announcement complicates this further — it's being acquired but is currently still a standalone company. Presenting it as a Salesforce product without differentiating it from native products is misleading.
